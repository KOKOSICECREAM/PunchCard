The Problem

You're building loyalty
on someone else's land.

Square, Toast, Belly, Stamp Me — every legacy loyalty platform owns the relationship between you and your customers. You're a tenant. When they raise prices, change terms, or shut down, your loyalty program disappears overnight.

Your customers' reward balances live on a server you don't control. That's not loyalty infrastructure — that's a subscription you can lose.

On-chain loyalty changes that equation permanently. When your program lives on Base mainnet, no vendor can take it from you. It belongs to you and your customers — forever.

Why Crypto

Not a gimmick.
A better architecture.

We're not putting blockchain on loyalty because it's trendy. We're doing it because a token on Base mainnet has properties that a database record never will.

Your customers' rewards have real value. Not points that expire. Not credits locked to your POS system. Actual tokens they hold in their own wallet — that can be traded, transferred, or redeemed anywhere that accepts them. That's a fundamentally different relationship between a customer and a reward.

Your program runs without a middleman. Traditional loyalty platforms charge monthly fees, own your customer data, and sit between you and every transaction. Smart contracts on Base mainnet automate the entire reward cycle — issuance, tracking, redemption — with no intermediary taking a cut and no server you depend on.

The data is yours and it's tamper-proof. Every transaction is recorded on a public, immutable ledger. You always know exactly how many tokens are in circulation, what's been redeemed, and what your customers hold. No vendor can manipulate or withhold that data.

No crypto experience required on your customers' end. The QR experience feels as simple as Apple Pay.

The PunchCard Promise

Built to protect what
you've already earned.

Every customer who walks back through your door made a choice. They chose you — your space, your people, your energy — over every other option they had. That's not a data point. That's trust. And trust deserves better than a loyalty program that lives on someone else's server, behind someone else's terms of service, generating someone else's revenue.

Every PunchCard deployment is built to the same standard — not because we're required to, but because the relationship it protects demands it.
Your customers' rewards can never be taken away.
When someone earns a token at your business, it's theirs. Not held in trust by a platform. Not subject to expiration policies or account closures. It lives in their wallet, permanently — because that's what a real reward feels like.
— All tokens deployed to immutable contracts. No admin keys. No freeze functions. Ever.
Nobody can change the rules after the fact.
You and your customers agree on how the program works on day one. Those terms are written into the contract and locked forever. No vendor can raise fees, reduce reward rates, or alter the program — because no vendor has that access.
— Reward rates set at deployment. Smart contracts are immutable once live. No exceptions.
The program is funded before it launches.
We require minimum seed liquidity before any deployment goes live. Your customers' rewards are backed by real value from the moment they earn their first token — not promises, not IOUs, not platform credits.
— Minimum $1,000 LP locked for one year. No deployment without it. No exceptions.
We enforce the standard. We don't benefit from it.
PunchCard takes no token allocation. We charge for the work — the build, the deployment, the expertise. The standard we enforce exists entirely to protect your customers and your business, not our position. That's the only way this works.
